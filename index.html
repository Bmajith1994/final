Best Writer

Anuk Arudpragasam

Anuk Arudpragasam is a celebrated novelist whose works explore themes of identity, trauma, and the aftermath of Sri Lanka’s civil war. His debut novel, "The Story of a Brief Marriage" (2016), received critical acclaim for its powerful portrayal of life during the war. His second novel, "A Passage North" (2021), was shortlisted for the Booker Prize, further cementing his reputation as a profound and introspective writer.

Shyam Selvadurai

Shyam Selvadurai is best known for his debut novel, "Funny Boy" (1994), which explores themes of identity, sexuality, and ethnic tensions in Sri Lanka. The novel was adapted into an internationally acclaimed film in 2020. Selvadurai's works, including "Cinnamon Gardens" and "The Hungry Ghosts," are celebrated for their lyrical prose and honest explorations of societal issues.

Shehan Karunatilaka is an internationally acclaimed author, best known for his Booker Prize-winning novel "The Seven Moons of Maali Almeida" (2022). His debut novel, "Chinaman: The Legend of Pradeep Mathew" (2010), is a witty and thought-provoking exploration of Sri Lanka’s love for cricket, set against a backdrop of politics and history. Karunatilaka's sharp prose and ability to blend humor with serious themes have won him a global audience.
